## Claims

- **[high, self_checked]** GAWM targets a specific failure mode of centralized world models under partial observability: "insufficient and unstable representation of global states," which produces a *time-varying* distribution mismatch between the world model's generated (imagined) samples and real samples — i.e., non-stationarity manifests here as training instability of the world model itself, not (only) as policy non-stationarity from co-learning agents. This is a usefully different framing from MARIE's agent-policy-non-stationarity framing.
- **[high, self_checked]** Architecture is RSSM-based (recurrent model + predictors), same dynamics-backbone lineage as MAMBA/MABL, but adds a Transformer specifically to *fuse* per-agent local-observation embeddings into one global representation — i.e., point 2 on our lit-map axis (per-agent inputs + centralized aggregation into a single shared latent), architecturally closer to MARIE's "aggregation" idea than to MABL's bi-level split, but built on RSSM rather than a fully Transformer-based world model.
- **[high, self_checked]** Explicitly follows CTDE: the *policy* network only ever consumes local per-agent observations (both at train and execution time), even though the *world model* is centralized/global — so the shared representation is a training-time signal only, similar in spirit to MABL's train-time-only sharing but achieved by decoupling world-model input scope from policy input scope rather than by a bi-level latent split.
- **[medium, self_checked]** Introduces "trend modeling" for team reward prediction (modeling the direction/shape of reward change rather than predicting exact reward values), motivated as reducing reward-modeling complexity and improving world-model training robustness — a concrete, checkable design choice, not yet verified against the primary text's ablations.
- **[medium, self_checked]** Reported to outperform MAPPO, QMIX, MAMBA, and MAG on SMAC (StarCraft Multi-Agent Challenge) scenarios — from secondary-source summary, not yet checked against GAWM's own results tables or map list.

## Method summary (best available from abstract + secondary sources)

Each agent produces a local-observation embedding; a Transformer fuses these per-agent embeddings into one global latent that the RSSM's recurrent world model conditions on, giving a single shared/global representation of team state despite partial observability. Reward is modeled as a trend (direction of change) rather than a precise scalar target. The policy network, per CTDE, only ever sees local observations — the global representation exists purely to make the *world model's* generative rollouts more consistent, not to leak global state into execution-time decision-making.

## Relevance to janus-chrysalis

A second concrete instance of "per-agent inputs, centralized/shared aggregation," giving axis point 2 two independent architectures (GAWM, MARIE) to compare rather than one — useful for the planned controlled sweep, since it lets us ask whether findings about aggregation-based non-stationarity mitigation are specific to MARIE's Transformer design or generalize to an RSSM-based aggregation scheme like GAWM's. The "trend modeling of reward instead of exact value" idea is also a candidate ablation independent of the sharing-topology axis (reward-signal precision vs. dynamics-signal precision).
