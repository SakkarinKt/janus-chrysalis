## Claims

- **[high, verified]** MMSA unifies joint state-action representation learning with imaginative rollouts: a world model trained with a variational autoencoder is augmented with a "state-action learned embedding" (SALE), injected into (a) the imagination module that forecasts future rollouts and (b) a joint-agent network whose per-agent action values are combined via a mixing network into a joint action-value function. Confirmed verbatim against the primary abstract.
- **[high, verified — resolves prior open question]** **The world model itself is shared/joint, not per-agent.** Primary text: "the joint agent network is a collection of all agents in the environment, and it implements the policy network that yields the joint policy π := (π¹,…,πᴺ)"; the VAE-based world model operates on aggregated joint state-action embeddings during training. However, execution is still CTDE-style: individual agents act on local observations at decision time, even though the world model that trained them was joint. **This changes the axis placement from the previous pass's guess**: MMSA is not really an "orthogonal, value-decomposition-only" axis point — its world model belongs on the shared/joint end of the sharing-topology axis (closest to axis point 1, "fully shared," though point 1's current description assumes policies condition on the shared model directly, whereas MMSA's policies use local obs + a mixing network on top). Recommend the lit-map's axis table entry for MMSA be revisited in the next increment to reflect "joint world model + CTDE execution + value-decomposition mixing," rather than "orthogonal axis."
- **[medium, verified — refined]** Non-stationarity is **not** absent from the paper as the previous pass speculated, but it's not a first-class motivation either: the introduction lists "non-stationarity issues posed by multi-agent systems" among the challenges motivating the work, but the paper's emphasis throughout is sample efficiency and richer joint representation learning, not mitigating co-learning non-stationarity specifically. A middle ground between "not mentioned" and "core design target."
- **[high, verified]** Evaluated on StarCraft II Micro-Management (**both SMAC v1 and SMACv2**), Multi-Agent MuJoCo, and Level-Based Foraging (LBF) — more precise than the previous pass's generic "SMAC."

## Method summary (best available from abstract + secondary sources)

A VAE-based world model — shared/joint across agents — learns joint state-action embeddings (SALE) during centralized training. During imagination, SALE-conditioned rollouts are generated; the resulting imagined trajectories feed a joint-agent value network whose individual agents' action-values are combined through a mixing network (QMIX-style) into a single joint action-value function used for policy improvement. Execution remains decentralized: individual agents act on local observations, consistent with CTDE. The stated benefit is that agents get a "richer understanding of how their choices influence collective outcomes" than value decomposition alone would give, because the value function is trained partly on model-generated (imagined) trajectories informed by the joint embedding.
