# MABL — Bi-Level Latent-Variable World Model for Sample-Efficient MARL

- **arXiv**: [2304.06011](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.06011) (v1: Apr 2023; published version at AAMAS 2024)
- **Venue**: AAMAS 2024 (23rd Intl. Conf. on Autonomous Agents and Multiagent Systems), Auckland, NZ — ACM DL 10.5555/3635637.3663049
- **Authors**: Aravind Venugopal, Stephanie Milani, Fei Fang, Balaraman Ravindran
- **Verification note**: same WebFetch outage as `mamba-2022.md` — abstract-level claims cross-checked across arXiv listing, IFAAMAS proceedings PDF, RBCDSAI publication page, and ACM DL. `self_checked`, confidence capped at `medium` below abstract level.

## Claims

- **[high, self_checked]** MABL's central complaint about prior latent-variable world models for MARL: they either (a) fail to encode global/centralized information into latent states (hurting sample efficiency), or (b) do encode it but then require centralized execution of the resulting policies — impractical under partial observability. This is stated near-verbatim across three independent sources.
- **[high, self_checked]** MABL's fix is a *bi-level* latent-variable world model: a global-info-bearing upper level used only during training, and a lower level that is all each agent uses at execution time — giving CTDE (centralized training, decentralized execution) for the world model itself, not just the policy.
- **[medium, self_checked]** MABL is designed to be algorithm-agnostic on the policy-learning side — "can be combined with any model-free MARL algorithm" — i.e. it is a world-model-as-data-augmentation module rather than a full actor-critic system like MAMBA.
- **[medium, self_checked]** Reported to surpass prior latent-variable world models (implicitly incl. MAMBA-style approaches) in sample efficiency and performance on SMAC, Flatland, and MAMuJoCo — three environment families, suggesting robustness across discrete and continuous action spaces. Not independently verified against MABL's results tables.

## Method summary (best available from abstract + secondary sources)

Two-level latent-variable world model. Upper-level latents are trained with access to global/centralized state (privileged information available only at train time in CTDE settings) and are dropped at execution. Lower-level (per-agent, execution-time) latents are trained to be informed by the upper level during training via some form of distillation/conditioning, so at test time an agent's own lower-level latent already carries a compressed trace of the global information it can no longer see. World model output feeds a downstream model-free MARL algorithm (algorithm-agnostic).

## Relevance to janus-chrysalis

Directly on-target for the "shared vs. per-agent world models" mission axis: MABL is explicitly a *hybrid* — shared/global at train time, per-agent at execution — which is a third point on that axis beyond "fully shared" and "fully independent" world models. Good candidate ablation arm: compare MAMBA-style per-agent-throughout vs. MABL-style bi-level vs. a fully-shared single world model, on the same laptop-scale env, to isolate where non-stationarity actually bites.
