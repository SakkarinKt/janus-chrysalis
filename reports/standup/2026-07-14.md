## Learned

- **[medium, self_checked]** The two backbones are not symmetric in implementation-robustness risk
  for a from-scratch TF.js build: RSSM's risk is "compose mature, existing ops correctly," while
  SSM/Mamba's risk starts one level lower — "the core primitive doesn't exist in this ecosystem at
  all." That asymmetry, not a marginal preference, is what makes this note's verdict fairly clear-cut
  despite being `self_checked` rather than `verified`.
- **[medium, self_checked]** ADR-0002 §7's original framing of the risk ("a subtle gradient bug in a
  hand-rolled recurrent cell") turns out to undersell how much of RSSM's stochastic path is already
  covered by stable, native TF.js primitives (`tf.stopGradient` alone covers the straight-through
  trick) — the genuinely novel code surface for RSSM is smaller than the gate's own wording implied.
- **[low, self_checked]** TF.js has no first-class numerical-gradient-check utility (unlike Python
  TF's `tf.test.compute_gradient`) — worth carrying into whichever future increment writes the cell's
  test suite, since it means that harness has to be hand-written, not imported.
