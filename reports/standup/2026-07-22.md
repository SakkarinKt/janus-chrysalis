## Learned

- **[high, self_checked, execution-confirmed]** Calling `tf.layers.gruCell`'s `.call()` method
  directly, one timestep at a time, avoids the `tensorflow/tfjs#1529`/`#3550`-style
  `tf.variableGrads` crash that `tf.layers.rnn`'s wrapper hits when chained across ≥2 timesteps in
  one differentiation trace. Confirmed via a from-scratch gradient-check up to 4 chained steps, then
  landed in `RSSMCell.step()` and covered by a committed test.
- **[high, self_checked, primary-source]** The crash's precise origin (not just "somewhere in
  tfjs-layers' RNN backward pass," §8's framing) is `rnn()`'s internal `tfc.unstack`/`tfc.stack`
  calls in `tfjs-layers/dist/layers/recurrent.js` — read directly from the installed package source,
  not inferred from the error message alone.
- **[medium, self_checked]** The week-3 spike's hard kill criterion (ADR-0002 decision 5) did **not**
  fire. The reviewer's instinct to try the wrapper-bypass before considering a custom-autograd
  fallback was correct — no new gradient math was needed, and (a) stays not-invoked.
- **[medium, self_checked]** The `fixedHard`-forces-a-constant-forward-value STE trap (documented at
  the standalone-estimator level in the 2026-07-20 entry) resurfaces one layer up: a multi-step
  finite-difference check built on `RSSMCell` can't validate `priorDense`'s own weights when
  `fixedHard` is used to keep the chain deterministic across repeated forward calls, since `sample`'s
  forward value doesn't depend on `priorDense` at all in that construction. Not a defect — `priorDense`'s
  gradient correctness is already covered by the existing single-step test (which reads out `probs`
  instead of chaining through `sample`) — just a gap in what this particular multi-step test
  construction can check, documented in both the test and the notes entry.
