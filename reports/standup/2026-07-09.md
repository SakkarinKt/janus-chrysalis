## Learned

- **[high, self_checked]** The human's two-sided gate rationale generalizes beyond this proposal: a
  validation gate that only checks for the *absence* of a failure mode (flat control) doesn't
  establish the instrument has *power* to detect the effect it exists to measure. Worth carrying
  into future proposal/gate design, not just this one.
- **[medium, self_checked]** The D2 approval separates two independent risk classes that were
  previously bundled in ADR-0002 §6's "working default recommendation": a *backend* risk (WebGPU's
  unresolved recurrent-gradient bug, #8590) that's cheap to avoid outright, versus an *architecture*
  risk (hand-rolled RSSM cell correctness) that's expensive to avoid and instead gets a verification
  gate (the robustness note) rather than a ban. Same underlying concern — silently-wrong gradients
  corrupting the measurement — two different mitigations depending on how costly avoidance is.
- **[medium, self_checked]** This run surfaced that "L2 promotion... recorded in a stand-up reply"
  (per `loop/GOAL.md`) and "adding dependencies... [needs] approval" (a separate boundary line) are
